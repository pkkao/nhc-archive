True, it is a mix of those things, but I suppose once you get to the “Seems to feel the same about me” part that Smiling mentioned, a large part of the dread half fades away. Sure, it might still pop up here and there, but it’s nowhere near as frequent. At least once the whole “I like you”/”I like you too” stuff is laid out on the table you’re more free to enjoy each others company without there being a constantly looming fear of saying something that gives away feelings that you might not want to give away. Like, the risk of scaring the other person away is gone and you’re just free to be who you are and they can do the same in kind. Which is also part of the fun of passing the admission mark, because when you’re both just free to be yourselves around each other, you get to learn all sorts of little bits and pieces about each other that you wouldn’t have known otherwise.
