cruel to be kind! but seriously if you ask the girl on valentine’s day once and for all and she rejects, then she does not like you, you should just move on. not that things are exactly equal on these parts but imagine if that girl came up to you on valentines day and asked you to do something with her. you wouldnt think of an excuse or get out of it, you would be super happy and say yes immediately. if she doesn’t do that, she does not feel the same way about you and who wants to be in a one-sided relationship like that? the worst kind of movement in a relationship like this is no movement. forwards or backwards, movement is good and it shows progression where you can take a viewpoint and think “ok this is either a) worth my time to continue with her or b) that rejection was an answer to that question I’ve been pondering for months and months and now I know she doesn’t like me so I can feel sad for a little bit and then move on to the next girl.”

just ask her out. and if you can’t build up the courage to do it, and she makes few/little signs of trying anything with you other than the odd “maybe we could do something” once a blue moon, just realise she’s just not into you, or even face the fact you just can’t get the confidence to do it (which is a personality trait like any other that some just don’t have), get upset about it, face the truth, and start eyeing up the next girl. you could still keep a friendship with the other girl, say hello when you see her around, whatever, and maybe something could happen down the line(?) but you have to get to a point where you think is this girl really worth all these months of my life just for the little glimpses of hope she gives me?

also like a lot of starry-eyed people, they tend to just ignore advice/negative comments because you can’t simply switch off “feelings” but sometimes you’ve got to just realise that they’ll be plenty more crushes in your life. take no prisoners. good luck!
