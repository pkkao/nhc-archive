So all that said… I have realized I have a little crush on this really cute girl who works at the thrift store we go to. I feel so guilty, like I’m the biggest hypocrite in the world–and I am–yet I don’t avoid her or so anything to make it go away. It’s not like we can go to a new thrift store anyway, but I also don’t want to. She’s kind, pretty, cheerful, helpful, we both get excited over the same kinds of cool finds. I have no idea if she’s lesbian, straight, bi, pan, asexual… she hits my radar as bi.

I think Mr. Paddlin has no idea. In fact, I think he sees us talking and just gets happy that I might be making a friend (I am very shy and reclusive). I try to only talk to her when he’s across the store, but of course a lot of times he’ll come up to the front and I won’t notice and then I get all flustered because a) I feel like a little girl getting caught doing something bad, b) I feel completely awful about it, and c) I know this is terrible but… I don’t want her to get the wrong impression and think I’m straight.

One person would realize they had jealousy issues, or one person would suddenly want them constantly, or it would just be this emotional disaster. The only couples who seemed able to pull it off were either in open relationships or polyamorous. Lastly, we both have jealousy issues (or so he tells me?) and he says that he doesn’t want to share me with anyone really and he especially doesn’t want that to be the moment he realizes he’s jealous of guys AND girls.

But… for a long time, I have felt like polyamory might actually be for me. The two people in my life needn’t be together, but should at least have a bond over me. I am a pretty damaged person who needs a LOT of love and care AND I also have endless amounts of love to give and work tirelessly to please the people I love and care about. I know what I want and need out of a relationship: the be nurtured, babied, protected, loved, to have my inner child and mental age respected, to be given respect and patience, and to be given discipline, boundaries, motivation, and encouragement. Most people think they know what they want out of a relationship, but they don’t know what they need.

I don’t know. 99.99999% chance nothing will come of it. Just feeling ultra guilty, thinking about what could be, and wanting to get it out to someone

kupomog (Joined Feb 2002, 37,972 posts) on 10-24-2014, 12:15 PM

The thing about online dating that I found good was that you’re sorta stuck having to pay attention to each other. You can’t get physical and unless you video chat, you tend to not put too much gravity/importance towards appearances and looks so much as you just care about what you are talking about. You have to communicate, you are FORCED to communicate, and it strengthened my relationship (and, you know, communication skills) a ton. It’s like seeing the real person inside all the time and connecting directly with that person. Of course, this is assuming you are talking to someone sincere and if that person is not sincere, it is not the fault of “online dating,” but the fault of the idiot who isn’t being truthful. This is where phone chatting comes in handy because it brings you closer and on a more personal level than text based chatting. Then you do have to build up the confidence of meeting in person but if your relationship/friendship online is strong, it shouldn’t be too much of a thing. For us it just instantly clicked. We got over the nervous pleasantries over dinner and went late night shopping for cereal or something after and it was all like normal, like it should be.

I mean obviously this can all be done in person, too, as I did with my ex but when you have massive anxieties (or physical ailments like I had at the time, severe peak of my chronic eczema which was wholly unattractive but my husband didn’t mind at the time because he rocks), online dating is a perfectly viable option. You will meet crappy people online you don’t mesh with - the same can happen in real life. Either way it’s all about how you approach it, being aware of what is being put out there as far as info or red flags, and knowing that there is a possibility that you will have to actually craft a relationship over a steady period of time and it might not just fall in your lap like nothing.
